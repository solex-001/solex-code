Color theory is both the science and art of using color. It explains how humans perceive color; how colors mix, match or clash; the subliminal messages colors communicate; and the methods used to replicate color.

Primary Colors

The primary colors are red, blue, and yellow. These colors cannot be created by mixing other colors. All other colors are derived from these three hues.

Primary Colors: Red, Blue, Yellow

In traditional color theory (used in paint and pigments), primary colors are the 3 pigment colors that cannot be mixed or formed by any combination of other colors. All other colors are derived from these 3 hues.

Secondary Colors

Secondary colors are created by mixing two primary colors. The secondary colors are green, orange, and purple.

Secondary Colors: Green, Orange, Purple

Here's how secondary colors are created:

  • Red + Yellow = Orange
  • Yellow + Blue = Green
  • Blue + Red = Purple

Tertiary Colors

Tertiary colors are created by mixing a primary color with a secondary color. This gives us even more color variations.

Tertiary Colors: Red-Orange, Yellow-Orange, Yellow-Green, etc.

Examples of tertiary colors include:

  • Red + Orange = Red-Orange
  • Yellow + Green = Yellow-Green
  • Blue + Purple = Blue-Purple
  • And many more combinations

Color Harmonies

Color harmonies are pleasing color schemes created according to their position on the color wheel.

Analogous, Complementary, and Triadic Schemes

Some common color harmonies include:

  • Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel
  • Analogous: Colors next to each other on the color wheel
  • Triadic: Three colors evenly spaced on the color wheel
  • Split-Complementary: A variation of the complementary scheme
